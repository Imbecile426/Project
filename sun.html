The Sun

Learn about the closest star to Earth.

Core

The Sun’s center, where nuclear fusion occurs, generating immense energy that powers the Sun and provides light and heat to our solar system.

Photosphere

The Sun’s visible surface, which emits the light we see on Earth. It has a temperature of around 5,500°C.

Chromosphere

A thin layer above the photosphere, visible during solar eclipses with a reddish glow due to hydrogen gas.

Corona

The outermost part of the Sun’s atmosphere, seen as a white halo during eclipses. It’s surprisingly hotter than the surface at around 1 million degrees Celsius.

Solar Flares

Powerful bursts of energy releasing intense radiation into space, affecting space weather and sometimes causing auroras on Earth.

Solar Wind

A stream of charged particles flowing from the Sun's corona, influencing planetary atmospheres and magnetic fields throughout the solar system.
