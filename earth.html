Planet Earth

Explore the layers and phenomena of our home planet.

Crust

The outermost layer of Earth, composed of solid rock and minerals. It’s where all terrestrial life exists.

Mantle

A thick layer beneath the crust made of semi-solid rock. Convection currents here drive tectonic plate movement.

Outer Core

A layer of molten iron and nickel, creating Earth's magnetic field through its flowing currents.

Inner Core

The solid, dense center of Earth, primarily composed of iron and nickel, reaching temperatures similar to the surface of the Sun.

Atmosphere

Earth’s protective layer of gases, essential for life, which shields us from harmful solar radiation and maintains climate.

Hydrosphere

The water component of Earth, including oceans, rivers, lakes, and glaciers, covering about 71% of Earth’s surface.
