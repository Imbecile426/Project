Earth's Moon

Explore the fascinating features and mysteries of Earth's only natural satellite.

Formation

Earth’s Moon is thought to have formed around 4.5 billion years ago from the debris left after a Mars-sized body collided with Earth.

Surface

The surface of the Moon is covered with craters, plains of basaltic rock, and dust. It has no atmosphere, so features remain largely unchanged over time.

Phases

The Moon goes through eight distinct phases, from new moon to full moon, caused by its changing position relative to Earth and the Sun.

Gravity

The Moon’s gravity is about one-sixth of Earth’s. This is why astronauts appear to jump higher and move more slowly on the lunar surface.

Tidal Locking

The Moon is tidally locked with Earth, meaning it always shows the same face to our planet. This happens because the Moon’s rotation period matches its orbital period.

Exploration

Humans first landed on the Moon during NASA’s Apollo 11 mission in 1969. There have been many missions since, but the Moon remains a key target for future exploration.
