SPACE

  1. Core

    The Sun’s center, where nuclear fusion occurs, generating immense energy.

  2. Photosphere

    The Sun’s visible surface, emitting the light we see on Earth.

  3. Chromosphere

    A thin layer above the photosphere, visible during solar eclipses with a reddish glow.

  4. Corona

    The outermost part of the Sun’s atmosphere, appearing as a white halo during eclipses.

  5. Sunspots

    Cooler, dark spots on the Sun’s surface, associated with magnetic activity.

  6. Solar Flares

    Sudden bursts of energy, releasing intense radiation into space.

  7. Solar Wind

    A stream of charged particles flowing from the corona, influencing space weather.

  1. Core

    The Earth's innermost layer, composed primarily of iron and nickel, generating the planet's magnetic field.

  2. Outer Core

    A molten layer surrounding the inner core, contributing to Earth's magnetic field through convection currents.

  3. Mantle

    The thick layer of hot, semi-solid rock between the Earth's crust and core, responsible for tectonic plate movement.

  4. Crust

    The Earth's outermost layer, composed of solid rock, including continents and ocean floors.

  5. Atmosphere

    A layer of gases surrounding the Earth, providing the necessary conditions for life, including oxygen, nitrogen, and carbon dioxide.

  6. Oceans

    The vast bodies of water covering around 71% of the Earth's surface, regulating climate and supporting marine life.

  7. Tectonic Plates

    Large slabs of the Earth's lithosphere that move and interact, causing earthquakes, volcanoes, and mountain formation.

  8. Magnetic Field

    Generated by the motion of the liquid outer core, protecting the Earth from harmful solar radiation.

  1. Cratered Surface

    The Moon’s surface is covered with craters from countless impacts over billions of years.

  2. Regolith

    A layer of dust and fragmented rock covering the Moon's surface, created by the constant bombardment of meteorites.

  3. Maria

    Large, dark, basaltic plains on the Moon’s surface, remnants of ancient volcanic activity.

  4. Lunar Atmosphere

    The Moon has a very thin atmosphere, made up mostly of helium, neon, and hydrogen.

  5. Lunar Eclipses

    Occur when the Earth’s shadow falls on the Moon, giving it a reddish hue.

  6. Phases of the Moon

    The Moon goes through different phases as it orbits Earth, including the new moon, first quarter, full moon, and last quarter.

  7. Moonlight

    The light we see from the Moon is actually reflected sunlight, often creating a soft glow in the night sky.
